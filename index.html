About me

My research lies primarily in the field of the economics of education, with a secondary focus on labour economics. I am particularly interested in gender and inequality, and my work centres on human capital formation and development across the life cycle, as well as its broader societal implications. I enjoy thinking about models and how to better translate lived experiences into mathematical forms. I am supervised by Professor Kevin Denny. I have a soft spot for macroeconomics, charts, and data visualisation. I also run a newsletter where I discuss the latest working papers, preprints, and published research in causal inference methods in economics. Proponent of #MarketsInEverything.
